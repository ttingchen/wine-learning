## Quick-Reference Cheat Sheet

### Sparkling Wines
| Wine | Country | Sweetness | Signature Flavor |
|------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Prosecco | Italy | Dry–Medium | Pear, floral |
| Cava | Spain | Dry | Apple, lemon |

### Fortified Wines
| Wine | Country | Sweetness | Key Trait |
|------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Sherry | Spain | Varies | White, many styles |
| Port | Portugal | Sweet | Red, full, high tannin |

### Red Wines (Light → Full)
| Wine | Country | Body | Tannin | Standout Note |
|------|---------|------|--------|---------------|
| Beaujolais | France | Light | Low | Fresh red fruit, unoaked |
| Côtes du Rhône | France | Medium | — | White pepper |
| Chianti | Italy | Medium | High | High acidity, clove |
| Rioja | Spain | Med–Full | — | Vanilla from oak |
| Châteauneuf-du-Pape | France | Full | — | High alcohol, clove |

|------|---------|------|-----------|---------------|
| Sauternes | France | White | Sweet | Apricot, honey, vanilla |

## Memory Tips

- **Two fortified wines**: Sherry (Spain, white, varies) vs Port (Portugal, red, sweet)
- **Two sparklings**: Prosecco (Italy, pear/floral) vs Cava (Spain, apple/lemon)
- **Clove = oak**: Both Chianti and Châteauneuf-du-Pape get clove from oak aging
- **Vanilla = oak**: Both Sauternes and Rioja get vanilla from oak aging
- **White pepper**: Côtes du Rhône's unique identifier
- **The only sweet still wines**: Sauternes (white) and White Zinfandel (rosé)
